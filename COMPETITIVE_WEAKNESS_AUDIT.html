The 5 things a rival would screenshot

Kannada Beku · audited 2 Jul 2026 on main + fix/bug-fixes-and-audits · codebase, foundation specs, and DB seed migrations reviewed

Headline: the app is in better mechanical shape than expected — all four issues from the 16 Jun flow audit are verified fixed, offline sync works, and there are no dead buttons. The real exposure is not broken flows; it is capabilities the app claims or implies but doesn't have, plus a total absence of any revenue path.

1Zero monetization — the business model is literally an open question

There is no monetization code anywhere: no IAP library, no RevenueCat/Stripe, no entitlement or tier field in stores/useUserStore.ts or the users table, no paywall, no ads. The business model sits as an unresolved [OPEN] item — "Monetisation model — free, freemium, paid?" (docs/foundation/SCOPE.md:110). Meanwhile the redesign handoff already promises "Free · no card · works offline" on the welcome sheet (spec_docs/Sameecha/playful_redesign_handoff/README.md:178), and the marketing-site questionnaire is still asking users what they'd pay (web/site-questionnaire.jsx:147–151).

Rival framing: not a screenshot — a runway chart. A funded competitor doesn't need to beat the product; they can outspend an app with no revenue mechanism and no decided path to one.

Cheapest counter: close the SCOPE.md decision. Even "free until Lesson 8 is doubled" is a stance; undecided is the only losing position.

2All Kannada audio is the phone's robot voice — and the app admits it on boot

Every sound in this listen-first language app comes from device text-to-speech (expo-speech via deviceTtsAudioService; e.g. src/games/dictation/utils/audioPlayer.ts:47). There is no recorded native-speaker audio at all. Worse, if the phone lacks a Kannada TTS voice — common on Android — the app opens with a dialog titled "Kannada voice not installed" telling the user to go download one in system settings (components/modals/instances/TTSUnavailableDialog.tsx).

Rival screenshot: that boot dialog, captioned "a listening app that ships without sound," next to their own human-recorded audio.

Cheapest counter: record the Lesson 0–2 core vocabulary with one native speaker and bundle it; keep TTS as fallback. Even partial human audio removes the screenshot.

3"Speak" practice is an honor-system button — with a ghost mic permission attached

The daily-goal rings show Listen / Speak / Practice, but the Speak ring increments when the learner taps an "I said it" button (components/lesson/PracticeWordsPhase.tsx:109–113, same in PracticePhrasesPhase.tsx:102). Nothing listens, nothing scores pronunciation. Yet the shipped Android manifest requests RECORD_AUDIO (app.json), and a mic permission dialog exists with the copy "We listen on-device while you speak the answer, then forget the recording" (components/modals/instances/PermissionDialog.tsx) — for a feature that doesn't exist; grep shows kind: 'mic' is never invoked.

Rival screenshot: the "I said it" button beside a Play-Store permission list showing microphone access — "it asks for your mic but speaking practice is a checkbox." Duolingo/Ling-class apps have real speech scoring.

Cheapest counter: drop RECORD_AUDIO and the mic dialog copy from the build today (removes the bad-faith angle); relabel or rethink the Speak ring separately.

4The whole curriculum is 8 lessons — and the app says "coming soon" when you hit the walls

Eight lesson slots exist and eight are authored (constants/lessons/plannedLessons.ts); there is no Lesson 9. A committed learner exhausts everything in days and lands on a Home card reading "All caught up — More lessons coming soon." (app/(tabs)/index.tsx:288). All four practice games show "Lesson N — coming soon" for any lesson whose game items aren't authored (OppositeGame.tsx:292, DictationGame.tsx:409, ConversationGame.tsx:393, QuickQuizGame.tsx:315), and old Image Match links show "Image Match has been retired." (app/(games)/[game]/[n].tsx:29–36).

Rival screenshot: a grid of the app's own "coming soon" and "retired" screens — "here's what's behind the download."

Cheapest counter: audit which lesson×game combinations actually surface a "coming soon" screen and either gate them from the UI or author the missing items; reframe the end-of-content card around review instead of absence.

5Nothing brings a finished user back — no review, no spaced repetition

Retention machinery is a streak counter plus one fixed daily notification ("Time for today's lesson." — lib/reminders.ts). There is no spaced-repetition or review system anywhere in the codebase, no streak protection, and once the 8 lessons are done the daily reminder points at a lesson that doesn't exist. Combined with #4, the product has a hard retention cliff at roughly week two, while every serious language-app rival leads with an SRS review loop.

Rival framing: a retention-curve comparison, or simply their review screen next to Kannada Beku's "All caught up" card.

Cheapest counter: a lightweight "review yesterday's words" deck reusing the existing practice components — the game runners and per-item progress data already exist to power it.
