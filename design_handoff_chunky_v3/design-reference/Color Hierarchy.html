Colour hierarchy

Two pigments, one ladder of neutrals. Red commands, gold rewards — and each colour family carries its own face, lip, pale surface and deep-text shade so combinations are never improvised.

The rules

Six decisions that keep the screens readable.

1
One red CTA per screen
Each screen gets exactly one action-red moment — the continue button, the play orb, the active tab. Everything else queues behind it.
2
Gold never competes
Gold marks progress and reward (bars, rings, streaks, done states). A gold button is always a secondary action next to a red one.
3
Pale tints are surfaces, not accents
Pale red and pale gold only ever appear as card fills, paired with their deep text shade — red on pale red, deep gold on pale gold.
4
Lips match their face
Every chunky element sits on a lip one shade deeper than its fill: action red → red lip, gold → gold lip, white → ink at 10%.
5
Pale red = urgent only
Pale red is reserved for emergency and error moments. If it isn't urgent, it isn't pink.
6
Neutrals do the layout
Sectioning comes from surface steps and the hairline ("felt, not seen") — never from coloured borders or new hues. No blue, green, teal or coral.
